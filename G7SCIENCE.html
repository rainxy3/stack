Solutions

1. What is dissolved in a solution?

a. Solute

b. Soluble

c. Solvent

d. Saturation


2. ____ will increase the movement or allows faster spreading of solute particles in the solvent.

a. Solute

b. Saturation

c. Solvent

d. Stirring


3. A mixture in which one substance is dissolved by another substance.

a. Suspension

b. Saturation

c. Solution

d. Solvent


4. All the following is the dependent variable the factor or condition that is

a. Measured or responding in an experiment

b. The change or result that occurs due to the independent variable

c. The “what will happen” in an experiment

d. Manipulated in the experiment


5. All of the following is the independent variable the factor or condition that is

a. Changed in an experiment directly caused by the experimenter

b. Measured or responding in an experiment

c. Manipulated in the experiment

d. The “what you do” in the experiment


6. A mixture of one phase only

a. Homogeneous mixture

b. Magic mixture

c. Heterogeneous mixture

d. Solid mixture


7. Solutions are homogeneous mixtures containing two or more substances called.

a. Solute and solvent

b. Saturation and Suspension

c. Polarity and Surface Tension

d. Saturated and Unsaturated


8. Why the solute cannot be separated from the solvent through filtration?

a. because sugar and water molecules move slower, and their interactions make the sugar molecules stay together longer.

b. because the positive area of one water molecule is attracted to the negative area of another.

c. because these are so small that they pass through the filter paper or cheesecloth.

d. because The mass of the solution would increase


9. Which dissolves faster in hot water

a. a. Coffee powder

b. Grain

c. Flour

d. Mayonnaise


10. What is solution?

a. is a vector expression of the displacement that an object or particle undergoes with respect to time.

b. is the phenomenon in which an object changes its position with respect to time.

c. is a substance in which a solute dissolve to produce a homogeneous mixture.

d. Is a homogeneous mixture of one or more solutes dissolved in a solvent.
