MATHEMATICS | GRADE 8

Reasoning

1. A simple flow of reasoning from the if-clause to the then-clause is called what?

a. Reasoning

b. If then statement

c. Simple Implication

d. None of the above


2. It is composed of two clauses: the if-clause and the then-clause.

a. Reasoning

b. If then statement

c. Simple Implication

d. None of the above


3. The p statement in the if-then statement is called the ____.

a. If-clause

b. Then- clause

c. Hypothesis

d. Conclusion


4. The q statement in the if-then statement is called the _____.

a. If-clause

b. Then- clause

c. Hypothesis

d. Conclusion


5. It is formed by joining two statements p and q using the words if and then.

a. Reasoning

b. If then statement

c. Simple implication

d. Conditional statements


6. It uses basic and/or general statements to arrive at a conclusion.

a. Inductive Reasoning

b. Deductive Reasoning

c. If then statement

d. None of the above


7. It uses specific examples to arrive at a general rule, generalizations, or conclusions.

a. Inductive Reasoning

b. Deductive Reasoning

c. If then statement

d. None of the above


8. To form this, interchange the hypothesis and the conclusion of the inverse statement.

a. Converse

b. Inverse

c. Contrapositive

d. None of the above


9. To form this, take the negation of both the hypothesis and the conclusion.

a. Converse

b. Inverse

c. Contrapositive

d. None of the above


10. To form this, interchange the hypothesis and the conclusion.

a. Converse

b. Inverse

c. Contrapositive

d. None of the above
