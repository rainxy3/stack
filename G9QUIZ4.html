Trigonometry

1. If a^2+b^2 = c^2 , what type of triangle do you have?

a. Acute Triangle

b. Obtuse Triangle

c. Right Triangle

d. Oblique Triangle


2. It is the angle from the horizontal line to the line of sight of the observer to the object below.

a. Angle of Elevation

b. Angle of Depression

c. Line of Sight

d. None of the above


3. It is one of the important branches in the history of mathematics that deals with the study of the relationship between the sides and angles of a right-angled triangle.

a. Geometry

b. Algebra

c. Trigonometry

d. None of the above


4. There are seven (7) trigonometric ratios.

a. True

b. False

c. Maybe

d. None of the above


5. It is the multiplicative inverse of the cosine.

a. Sine

b. Secant

c. Cotangent

d. Cosecant


6. It is the angle from the horizontal to the line of sight of the observer to the object above it

a. Angle of Elevation

b. Angle of Depression

c. Line of Sight

d. None of the above


7. It is the multiplicative inverse of the sine.

a. Sine

b. Secant

c. Cotangent

d. Cosecant


8. The ratio in a right triangle of adjacent to hypotenuse is ____.

a. Sine

b. Tangent

c. Secant

d. Cosine


9. It is the multiplicative inverse of the tangent.

a. Sine

b. Secant

c. Cotangent

d. Cosecant


10. It is the side next to the reference angle in a right triangle

a. Obtuse Triangle

b. Adjacent Side

c. Opposite Side

d. Acute Triangle
